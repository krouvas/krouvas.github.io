Monopoly Game Analysis     - 12/2019

In this project first a Python script was used for the simulation of a set of rounds on a realistic Monopoly board and to produce useful data.
These data include Priority numbers to help with deciding which property to buy and also the probability of finding a piece at each tile at the end of each round.
Finally, an R script was used to present the probabilities of each tile in a heatmap of the whole Monopoly board as displayed below.

Science Fair Gunpowder demonstration     - 02/2019

I was allowed to plan the approach and produce a small sample of Gunpowder to be demonstrated in the GC School of Careers 2018 Science Fair. Potassium Nitrate, Sulphur and ground Charcoal in the right proportions were mixed. An 99% Alcohol solution was used to wet the sample for safety reasons and a mortar and pestle were used to grind and homogenise the mixture until I was satisfied with the result. Afterwards, the Gunpowder paste was forced through a mesh fabric to form granules which where dried over several days in a tray with paper towels and away from sources of ignition. The media below show the measured components, the grinding process, the drying process, and the final yield of this project. A burn test of a single wet granule is included as well. Tesla Radio     - 05/2016

This Tesla Radio was made by adapting components provided from a radio building kit. The schematics for it were found online and luckily all the required components except one were found in the radio building kit. The missing component was a 50 kÎ© resistor which was constructed using a strip of paper that was vigorously rubbed with a pencil lead to produce a semiconducting line and tuned depending on the separation of the 2 paperclip electrodes. This homemade resistor was tightly wound with electrical tape and can be seen along with the rest of the radio in the image below. Additionally, signal recordings of a thunderstorm, a phone call and a piezo igniter sparking are included.
